# Steam Page Optimization Guide [2026]
Source: https://presskit.gg/field-guides/steam-page-optimization-guide
Date: 2026

## Core Philosophy

Steam store page = primary sales tool. Most wishlists happen without players trying a demo. People browse the page, evaluate visuals and text, then decide.

## Capsule Art Requirements

| Type | Size | Context |
|------|------|---------|
| Header | 920×430px | Top of store page, Big Picture |
| Small | 462×174px | Search results, lists (auto-generates 120×45px) |
| Main | 1232×706px | Homepage carousel |
| Vertical | 748×896px | Seasonal sales |

Design rules:
- Logo readable at smallest sizes
- Capsule = artwork + game title only (no review quotes, awards, marketing text)
- High contrast between logo and background
- Simple composition with one focal point

## Screenshots Strategy

- Upload 8–12 screenshots (Steam minimum: 5)
- First screenshot appears in search results and hover previews → show core gameplay, not title screens
- Include UI elements
- At least 4 must be suitable-for-all-ages to appear in hover previews
- Prohibited: concept art, pre-rendered stills, marketing copy, award badges

## Description Structure

**Short Description (300 chars):** Open with genre/hook + unique mechanic.
Example (Balatro): "The poker roguelike. Balatro is a hypnotically satisfying deckbuilder where you play illegal poker hands, discover game-changing jokers, and trigger satisfying combos."

**Long Description:**
- Lead with animated GIF or compelling imagery
- One-paragraph pitch
- Feature lists with bold headers
- 3–4 additional GIFs showing different gameplay
- No external links, fake UI mimicry, or cross-promotion widgets

## Tags Strategy

- Apply all 20 available tag slots
- Top 5 tags carry most algorithmic weight
- Specific rare tags > generic tags for recommendations
- Avoid "Indie" or "Singleplayer" in top priority slots
- Research: examine tags on 5–10 successful peer games

Tags drive: Discovery Queue, "More Like This" algorithms, tag-based filtering.

## Trailer Optimization

- List gameplay trailer first (not cinematics or logos)
- Open with gameplay within first 5 seconds
- Must work muted
- Optimal length: 60–90 seconds
- Specs: 1920×1080, 30/60fps, 5000+ Kbps minimum
- Steam auto-generates 6-second "microtrailer" from first trailer for hover previews
- Moving gameplay from second 18 to second 1 → +31% wishlist conversion in 2 weeks (case study)

## Coming Soon Strategy

- Launch Coming Soon page as early as marketing materials are ready
- No strong downside to long lead time
- Wishlists collected early = same conversion rate as late wishlists
- Minimum 2-week Coming Soon period required before release

## Review Mechanics

- Only 2.4% of games on Steam in 2024 accumulated 1,000 reviews
- 1,000 reviews threshold = algorithmic support + Daily Deal eligibility
- 20%+ discount triggers wishlist notification emails (below 20% = no email)
- Respond to negative reviews sparingly — developer responses amplify visibility of complaints

## Early Access

Steam requires questionnaire: why EA, duration, how full version differs, current state, pricing changes. EA → Full Release = second launch window with renewed algorithmic visibility.

## Localization

60%+ of Steam users run the platform in a non-English language. Store page localization (without full game translation) provides measurable visibility improvement.

## Genre Success Rates (2024 data)

- Open world survival craft: 24.5% success rate reaching 1,000 reviews
- 2D platformers: 0.25%

Optimization is a multiplier, not salvation. Success requires: (1) game fitting a real audience, (2) tight store page assets + accurate tags, (3) iterating on conversion data.
