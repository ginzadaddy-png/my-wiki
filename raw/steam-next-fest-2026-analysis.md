# Making Sense of the February 2026 Steam Next Fest
Source: https://howtomarketagame.com/2026/04/13/making-sense-of-the-february-2026-steam-next-fest/
Date: 2026-04

## Wishlist Performance Tiers

- **Struggling**: 1,000 wishlists or less
- **Mid-tier**: 2,000–3,000 wishlists with decent streamer coverage
- **Top performers**: ~15,000 wishlists
- **Breakout hits**: 30,000–45,000 wishlists (3–5 games per fest)

## Algorithm Changes & Visibility Trends

October 2024: Valve implemented a "machine learning visibility algorithm" providing baseline exposure during the first two days. Initial calibration was poor — reduced visibility for all tiers. February 2025 and 2026 showed recalibration; upper tiers recovered, lower tiers remain flat.

**Key data point**: Crashlands 2 (June 2024) — 6.2M impressions → 15,987 wishlists. How Many Dudes? (February 2026) — 14,740 wishlists from only 645,745 impressions. Interpretation: Valve tuned algorithm to better match players to their preferences.

## Rise of Non-Wishlisting Players

Median player counts increased across all tiers in February 2026. Growth concentrated in top-performing co-op games where players engage without wishlisting. Attributed to casual gamers discovering festival through trending "friend-slop" titles.

## Demo Launch Timing Strategy

Shadow-dropping demos before the festival is NOT recommended.

- Games launching demos 1+ month before consistently outperformed those launching within 30 days
- Of 10 top-earning developers surveyed, only one used the surprise-drop approach
- Most top performers had demos available for months before the festival

## Recommended Strategy

- Release demos months in advance
- Build momentum through marketing in the weeks preceding the festival
- Maximize wishlists through sustained effort
- Participate in the final festival before official launch

Despite increased competition and algorithmic changes, "the performance matched the years post October 2024" — foundational strategy remains effective.
